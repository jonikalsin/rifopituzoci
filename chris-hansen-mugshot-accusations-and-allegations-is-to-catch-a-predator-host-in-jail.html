Accusations and Allegations Against Chris Hansen

Throughout his career, Chris Hansen has been the target of several claims and charges, both connected to and unrelated to his work on “To Catch a Predator.” He was charged with not paying him for his work on a Kickstarter effort to restart “To Catch a Predator” in 2018 by a former worker.

The worker said that Hansen made a commitment to pay him a portion of the monies generated but then broke it. Hansen refuted the accusations, claiming that he had paid the worker what was due.

In January 2019, Hansen was charged with larceny as a consequence of a check that bounced with a supplier of promotional products. Hansen brought himself up to Connecticut police on January 14 for allegedly faking checks for promotional items worth over $13,000, according to the Stamford Police. The charges were later withdrawn.

The journalist was supposed to show up and give testimony on an undercover operation in October 2020 before a courtroom in Michigan in July 2021. A warrant was issued in July 2021 for Hansen’s arrest after he failed to show up for court. Later, when he brought himself up, he said that he had skipped court due to a misunderstanding that was being resolved.
